ALASKA

An Alaska cruise is the most convenient way to experience the main beauties of this vast and still wild territory of over 1.7 million km2, which could fit 178 out of the world's 194 nations. In its expansive and almost untouched spaces, only 720,000 people live, allowing for the preservation of natural wonders such as its nature reserves, glaciers, lakes, and rivers, with their incredible wildlife and flora.

ORLANDO, EE.UU

The city of Orlando is fantastic for enjoying vacations both for children and adults. Its amusement parks such as Magic Kingdom, Disney-MGM Studios, and Universal Studios are among the best in the world, making it a coveted destination for millions of tourists.

PUNTA CANA, THE DOMINICAN REPUBLIC

is a world-renowned tourist destination. Located on the east coast of the country, this beautiful region offers white sandy beaches, crystal-clear waters, and a tropical climate that make it a vacation paradise.In addition to its stunning beaches, Punta Cana offers a vibrant nightlife, quality restaurants with delicious local and international cuisine, world-class golf courses, and the opportunity to explore Dominican culture through excursions to nearby towns.

ROMA, ITALY

Rome annually welcomes over 7 million foreign tourists who want to explore the monuments of what was once the Roman Empire. The Colosseum, the Pantheon, and the Roman Forum are symbols of the magnificence of that era when the city was the "capital of the world." The talent of the imperial builders was inherited by artists of later periods, especially during the Renaissance, resulting in gems such as St. Peter's Basilica, the Archbasilica of St. John Lateran, and the national monument to Victor Emmanuel II.

Maui, Hawai, EE.UU

Maui is the second largest island in Hawaii but the most popular among tourists due to its 50 km of paradisiacal beaches, lush jungles, and magnificent golf courses. It is a true beauty. Paris is among the top 10 most tourist cities in the world. The Eiffel Tower alone attracts over 7 million people every year. It's difficult to decide what is more important in the French capital: its historical and artistic landmarks or its gastronomic art and restaurants. Iceland

There is no better place in Europe to admire the polar auroras, the midnight sun, and the white nights than Iceland. The geologically rich areas of Thingvellir and Skaftafell, the glaciers with their fascinating ice formations, Lake Myvatn, the waterfalls of Godafoss and Gullfoss, and the geothermal spas, especially the Blue Lagoon, showcase landscapes of breathtaking beauty

New Zealand

This oceanic archipelago has grown as a tourist destination due to its volcanoes, idyllic beaches, and cities with a high standard of living. Auckland is its most populous city and offers beautiful beaches, lush coastlines, and charming bays. Wellington, its capital, stands out for its vibrant nightlife and rich culture. In the town of Whakatane, you can visit Whakaari, an active marine volcano.
